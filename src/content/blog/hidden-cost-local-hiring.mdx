# The Hidden Cost of Local Hiring (And How Remote Solves It)

When a VP of Engineering looks at their budget for a new Senior React Developer in a major US tech hub, they typically pencil in $150,000 to $180,000. But that number is a dangerous illusion. 

The true cost of hiring a local, full-time employee (FTE) is dramatically higher once you factor in the "hidden costs" of traditional employment. Let's break down the real numbers and explore how a dedicated remote staffing model completely changes the financial equation.

## The True Cost Breakdown of a $150k Local Hire

If the base salary is $150,000, here is what the company is actually paying:

1. **Recruitment Costs (20-25%):** Using an external agency to find a senior developer typically costs 20% of their first-year salary. **Cost: $30,000.**
2. **Benefits & Taxes (30%):** Healthcare, 401k matching, employer taxes, and PTO typically add 30% to the base salary. **Cost: $45,000.**
3. **Office Space & Overhead:** Even in a hybrid model, the cost of desk space, snacks, and utilities in cities like SF or NY averages $10,000 per employee annually. **Cost: $10,000.**
4. **Hardware & Software:** A high-end MacBook Pro, monitors, and SaaS licenses. **Cost: $4,000.**
5. **Onboarding & Lost Productivity:** It takes an average of 3-4 months to fill a senior role. That is a quarter of a year where product development is stalled. (Hard to quantify, but easily tens of thousands in lost revenue).

**Total First-Year Cost:** ~$239,000 (nearly 60% higher than the base salary).
