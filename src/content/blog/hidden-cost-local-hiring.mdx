# The Hidden Cost of Local Hiring (And How Remote Solves It)

When a VP of Engineering looks at their budget for a new Senior React Developer in a major US tech hub, they typically pencil in $150,000 to $180,000. But that number is a dangerous illusion.

The true cost of hiring a local, full-time employee (FTE) is dramatically higher once you factor in the "hidden costs" of traditional employment. Let's break down the real numbers and explore how a dedicated remote staffing model completely changes the financial equation.

## The True Cost Breakdown of a $150K Local Hire

If the base salary is $150,000, here is what the company is actually paying:

### 1. Recruitment Costs (20–25%)

Using an external agency to find a senior developer typically costs 20% of their first-year salary. Even if you hire in-house recruiters, you're paying their salaries, LinkedIn Recruiter licences ($10K+/year per seat), and job board fees. And this cost is *per hire* — if your first-choice candidate declines the offer, you start over.

**Cost: $30,000.**

### 2. Benefits and Taxes (30%)

Healthcare premiums, 401(k) matching, employer-side payroll taxes (FICA, FUTA, state unemployment), paid time off, parental leave, and equity compensation. In competitive markets like San Francisco and New York, companies are also offering wellness stipends, education budgets, and home-office allowances to attract top talent.

**Cost: $45,000.**

### 3. Office Space and Overhead

Even in a hybrid model, the cost of maintaining desk space, meeting rooms, snacks, and utilities in cities like San Francisco or New York averages $10,000–$15,000 per employee annually. If you're fully in-office, that number can exceed $20,000 in premium locations.

**Cost: $10,000–$15,000.**

### 4. Hardware and Software

A high-end MacBook Pro, external monitors, standing desk, ergonomic chair, and SaaS licences (IDE, design tools, security tooling, project management). Most companies refresh hardware every 3 years, so the annualised cost is higher than the upfront number suggests.

**Cost: $4,000–$6,000.**

### 5. Onboarding and Lost Productivity

This is the hidden cost that nobody budgets for. It takes an average of 3–4 months to fill a senior engineering role. That is a quarter of a year where product development is stalled, deadlines slip, and existing team members absorb extra work — increasing their burnout risk and raising the probability that *they* leave too. Once hired, it takes another 2–3 months for the new engineer to reach full productivity. The total productivity gap can span 6+ months.

**Cost: Easily $50,000+ in delayed revenue and team drag (conservatively).**

### The Real Total

**Total First-Year Cost: $239,000–$296,000** — that's 60–97% higher than the base salary number your finance team approved. And if the hire doesn't work out (industry data suggests 20% of engineering hires leave within the first year), you get to pay these costs all over again.
